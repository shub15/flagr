# Employee Contract Checklist - Reference Playbook for Strategist Agent

## Essential Clauses

### Job Details
- Job title and detailed role description
- Department and reporting structure
- Probation period (typically 3-6 months as per Indian law)
- Start date and work location

### Compensation & Benefits
- Base salary (monthly/annual)
- Payment schedule (must be monthly as per Payment of Wages Act)
- Overtime compensation policy
- Annual increment structure
- Bonus/performance incentives
- Provident Fund (PF) - mandatory if company has 20+ employees
- Employee State Insurance (ESI) - mandatory if salary < ₹21,000
- Gratuity provisions (after 5 years as per Payment of Gratuity Act)
- Health insurance coverage
- Other benefits (food, transport, phone, internet)

### Working Hours & Leave
- Working hours per day/week (max 48 hours/week as per Factories Act)
- Overtime rules and compensation
- Paid leave entitlements:
  - Casual Leave (CL)
  - Sick Leave (SL)
  - Earned Leave/Privilege Leave (EL/PL) - minimum 1 day per 20 days worked
- Maternity Leave (26 weeks as per Maternity Benefit Act)
- Paternity Leave
- Public holidays

### Termination & Notice
- Notice period (both employer and employee) - typically 30-90 days
- Grounds for termination
- Severance/compensation on termination
- Exit formalities and handover process

### Intellectual Property
- Ownership of work created during employment
- Scope should be limited to work-related creation
- Post-employment IP rights

### Confidentiality & Non-Disclosure
- Scope of confidential information (should be reasonable)
- Duration of confidentiality obligations
- Permitted disclosures

### Non-Compete & Non-Solicitation
- Geographic scope (should be reasonable)
- Duration (typically 6-12 months, must not be unreasonable restraint of trade)
- Activities restricted
- Note: Must comply with Indian Contract Act Section 27 (restraint of trade)

### Dispute Resolution
- Grievance redressal mechanism
- Arbitration/mediation clause
- Jurisdiction (courts of specific city)

### Statutory Compliance
- Statement of compliance with:
  - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947
  - Payment of Wages Act, 1936
  - Minimum Wages Act, 1948
  - Payment of Bonus Act, 1965
  - Employees' Provident Funds Act, 1952
  - Other applicable labour laws

### Modern Workplace Provisions
- Work from home policy
- Equipment/technology stipend
- Professional development/training budget
- Performance review process

## Red Flags to Watch For

- Probation period > 6 months
- Notice period disparity (employer gets shorter notice than employee)
- No mention of statutory benefits (PF/ESI/Gratuity)
- Overly broad non-compete (entire industry, nationwide, > 2 years)
- IP assignment of personal projects/pre-existing work
- Unlimited confidentiality scope
- No grievance redressal mechanism
- Missing leave policy details
- Salary below minimum wage for the state/industry

# Example Contract for Testing
# This is a sample employment contract with intentional issues for testing the Legal Advisor system

EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT

This Agreement is entered into on January 1, 2024 between:

EMPLOYER: TechCorp India Pvt Ltd
EMPLOYEE: John Doe

1. POSITION AND DUTIES
The Employee is hired as Software Engineer and will report to the Engineering Manager.

2. COMPENSATION
Monthly salary: ₹60,000 (Sixty Thousand Rupees)

3. WORKING HOURS
The Employee agrees to work as per company requirements, including weekends when necessary.

4. PROBATION
The probation period is 12 months. During this time, employment can be terminated immediately without notice.

5. TERMINATION
Either party may terminate this agreement with 15 days notice. The company reserves the right to terminate immediately for any reason.

6. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
All work, ideas, inventions, and creations by the Employee, whether during work hours or personal time, shall belong to the Company.

7. NON-COMPETE
The Employee agrees not to work in the software industry in India for 3 years after leaving the company.

8. CONFIDENTIALITY
The Employee shall not disclose any information about the company, its clients, or business operations, forever.

9. LIABILITIES
The Employee accepts full liability for any losses incurred by the company during their employment.

10. GOVERNING LAW
This agreement shall be governed by the laws of India.

___________________                    ___________________
Employer Signature                      Employee Signature


ISSUES IN THIS CONTRACT (for testing):
- Probation period too long (12 months vs 3-6 months standard)
- No mention of PF/ESI/statutory benefits
- Immediate termination during probation (should have notice)
- Weak notice period (15 days vs 30-90 days)
- Overly broad IP assignment (includes personal time)
- Unreasonable non-compete (3 years, entire industry, nationwide)
- Unlimited confidentiality (should have reasonable scope)
- Employee liability for company losses (illegal)
- No mention of leave policy
- No overtime compensation
- Working hours not defined (mentions weekends)
- Missing: health insurance, gratuity details
- Missing: dispute resolution mechanism
- Missing: equipment/WFH policy
